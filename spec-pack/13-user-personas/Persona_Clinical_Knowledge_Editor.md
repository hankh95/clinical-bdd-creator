# Persona: Clinical Knowledge Editor

**Name**: Dr. Eleanor Hughes (fictional exemplar)  
**Role/Job Title**: Clinical Knowledge Editor (Topic Lead)  
**Workplace/Organization**: BMJ Best Practice  
**Location**: London, UK

---

## Basic Information

- Age: 40--55
- Education/Certifications: MD, MPH, MSc in Clinical Informatics
- Years of Experience: 12 years in clinical care, 8 in guideline governance

## Demographics

- Specialty: Internal Medicine, Endocrinology (example: Diabetes)
- Informatics Experience: Governance, knowledge lifecycle, standards alignment (HL7/FHIR, AJMQ)
- Technical Skills: Strong awareness of computable guideline models and provenance tracking
- Typical Tasks: Set topic roadmap, delegate authoring/review assignments, ensure multi-layer alignment

## Background/Expertise

- Primary Goals: Deliver authoritative, computable guideline content across all layers
- Motivations: Commitment to quality governance, ensuring trust in BMJ content

## Goals/Motivations

- Needs: Cross-role dashboards, dependency management across layers, governance tools
- Pain Points: Aligning multiple contributors, keeping pace with evolving evidence

## Activities

- Assigns topics to authors, tracks progress, oversees review cycles
- Collaboration: Manages authors, terminologists, technical authors, and reviewers
- Tools: Editorial workflow management, dashboards, Git-based pipelines

## Workflows/Behaviors

- Proficiency: High in governance and workflow management
- Support Needed: Integrated dashboards, change management tools

## Technology Comfort

> "I'm responsible for ensuring the right people work on the right layers and that the knowledge is correct, consistent, and published on time."
