# Persona: Clinical Informaticist

**Name**: Dr. Mark Lee (fictional exemplar)  
**Role/Job Title**: Clinical Informaticist  
**Workplace/Organization**: Health System  
**Location**: Chicago, IL

---

## Basic Information

- Age: 38--60
- Education/Certifications: MD/DO plus MS in Biomedical Informatics or Clinical Informatics
- Years of Experience: 10--20 in clinical practice and informatics

## Demographics

- Specialty: Clinical informatics, EHR/CDS implementation, guideline localization
- Informatics Experience: Advanced—FHIR, HL7, CDS standards, clinical workflow mapping
- Technical Skills: Requirement analysis, standards adaptation, evidence grading, exception management
- Typical Tasks: Select and localize guidelines for CDS, validate clinical fidelity, manage exceptions and overrides

## Background/Expertise

- Primary Goals: Ensure clinical fidelity and local relevance in CDS deployments
- Motivations: Patient safety, clinician workflow optimization, evidence-based care

## Goals/Motivations

- Needs: Provenance data, evidence grading clarity, contraindications, clinical rationale for CDS recommendations
- Pain Points: Incomplete evidence, ambiguous contraindications, resistance from clinicians due to workflow mismatch

## Activities

- Maps guidelines to local workflows, oversees validation and testing, analyzes override causes
- Collaboration: Works with CDS architects, product managers, frontline clinicians, and implementation teams
- Tools: EHR, CDS testing sandbox, workflow mapping tools, clinical analytics dashboards

## Workflows/Behaviors

- Proficiency: High with EHR customization and workflow integration, moderate to high with CDS and evidence review
- Support Needed: Transparent provenance documentation, local exception rules, regular updates on evidence base

## Technology Comfort

> "A CDS system is only as good as its fit to clinical reality—effective informatics bridges technical design with patient care."
