# Persona: Clinical Knowledge Author

**Name**: Dr. Sarah Patel (fictional exemplar)  
**Role/Job Title**: Clinical Knowledge Author  
**Workplace/Organization**: BMJ Best Practice authoring team  
**Location**: London, UK

---

## Basic Information

- Age: 30--45
- Education/Certifications: MD, MPH, Fellowship in Evidence-Based Medicine
- Years of Experience: 5--10 years clinical practice, 2--5 years in editorial guideline work

## Demographics

- Clinical Specialty: Internal Medicine (subspecialty depending on topic)
- Informatics Experience: Familiar with evidence grading, structured authoring workflows
- Technical Skills: Moderate, basic FHIR awareness, guideline tagging standards
- Typical Tasks: Drafting clinical best practice content (Layer 0), mapping inclusion/exclusion criteria into structured semantic items (Layer 1)

## Background/Expertise

- Primary Goals: Ensure clinical accuracy and readability of best practice content, contribute to evidence-based medicine translation
- Motivations: Passion for teaching and codifying clinical knowledge for broad use

## Goals/Motivations

- Needs: Clear editorial guidance, structured authoring templates, access to updated references (NCCN, ADA, AJMQ)
- Pain Points: Rapid evidence changes, balancing readability with computability

## Activities

- Drafts Layer 0 prose, maps to Layer 1 structured lists
- Collaboration: Works closely with editors, technical authors, and reviewers
- Tools: Authoring platform, reference libraries, citation managers

## Workflows/Behaviors

- Proficiency: Moderate, focused more on content than tech
- Support Needed: Training in semantic tagging and computable formats

## Technology Comfort

> "I focus on translating clinical best practice into clear text and structured items --- so that others can build computable logic on top of it."
