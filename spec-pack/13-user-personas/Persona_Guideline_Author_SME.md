# Persona: Guideline Author (Subject Matter Expert)

**Name**: Dr. Elena Garcia (fictional exemplar)  
**Role/Job Title**: Guideline Author (SME)  
**Workplace/Organization**: International Specialty Society / Medical Publisher  
**Location**: Barcelona, Spain

---

## Basic Information

- Age: 40--65
- Education/Certifications: MD, subspecialty fellowship (e.g., endocrinology, cardiology), advanced training in evidence synthesis
- Years of Experience: 15--30 in clinical practice and scholarly guideline authorship

## Demographics

- Specialty: High-level expert in chosen clinical field (e.g., diabetes, oncology, infectious diseases)
- Informatics Experience: Moderate—familiar with guideline authoring platforms, grading systems, and computable knowledge approaches
- Technical Skills: Evidence synthesis, recommendation grading (GRADE, AJMQ), systematic literature review, structured writing
- Typical Tasks: Define clinical questions, synthesize evidence, draft recommendations and rationale, respond to peer reviews

## Background/Expertise

- Primary Goals: Develop accurate, up-to-date, and actionable clinical guidance reflecting best evidence
- Motivations: Improving global standards of care, advancing clinical knowledge, influencing health outcomes at scale

## Goals/Motivations

- Needs: Access to comprehensive and current literature, structured authoring templates, clarity on grading frameworks, transparency on conflicts of interest and rationale changes
- Pain Points: Rapid evidence evolution, reconciling conflicting studies and guidelines, balancing clarity for clinicians and computable formats

## Activities

- Drafts and updates guideline narrative and recommendations, grades evidence strength, revises drafts post-review, participates in consensus panels
- Collaboration: Works closely with methodologists, knowledge editors, informatics leads, external clinical reviewers
- Tools: Guideline authoring platforms (e.g., GRADEpro), citation/reference managers, structured data export tools for computable guidance

## Workflows/Behaviors

- Proficiency: High with guideline authoring and clinical synthesizing, moderate with knowledge representation and structured templates
- Support Needed: Editorial support, regular literature updates, training on new knowledge representation standards

## Technology Comfort

> "Every recommendation is a chance to improve patient care—our guidance must be both clinically sound and ready for integration into tomorrow’s CDS systems."
